Top 20 Tourist places in Nagaland


Go to bottom V

  1. Kohima




    2. One of the most charming places to visit in Nagaland is the capital city – Kohima. Despite being exposed to the modern world, Kohima maintained its natural beauty and has its own charismatic elegance. Apart from its scenic views, Kohima also reminds the time of world war II. It has witnessed the Burma attack of 1944. And to pay respect to the fallen soldiers, Kohima War Cemetery was constructed. There are more than 1400 graves on the premises. This small town is dominated by the Angami tribe. Furthermore, one can enjoy hiking, trekking, and camping amidst the breath-taking mountains and forests. Don’t forget to visit Naga heritage village and Japfu peak among others.


  2. Dimapur




    3. Perfect for nature lovers and history buffs, Dimapur is one of the major tourist attractions places to visit in Nagaland. The famous place to visit in this quaint town is most definitely the Kachari Ruins, constructed in the 13th century, which is the amalgamation of history and nature. Ahom invasion is quite evident in the ruins. If you want to experience the tranquillity of nature then do visit the Kuki Dolong village, Seithekima village, Nichuguard village are some of the places to visit in Dimapur. Spend some time in peace far away from the chaos of the city. There are many places to shop the finest artifacts of Naga but Ruzaphema is by far the best place to shop. It is located at a distance of 5km from the capital city Kohima.


  3. Mokokchung




    4. Home to the Ao Naga tribe, Mokokchung is a fun town to visit just like its quirky name. Mokokchung has many wonderful unprecedented traditions and colourful festivals which you can enjoy to the core of your heart. Surrounded by wonderful muffin mountains and glistening streams, this small town is definitely worth the visit. While you’re at it, do visit the mystical caves of Longkhum and Langpangkong and get lost in the aura of the old-world charm.


  4. Mon




    5. Perched at a height of 897m above sea level, Mon is home to the Konyak Naga tribe. They are the descendants of Noah or at least this is what is believed. Mon is one of the famous tourist places of Nagaland. It offers rich tribal culture and heritage along with a mystifying atmosphere, it is said to be the most fascinating places to visit in Nagaland. We assure you that you’ll spend the most glorifying moments of your life during your stay here.


  5. Wokha




    6. The lush greenery and the hills in the backdrop look lively and much prettier here. Wokha is home to the Lotha tribe. Along with their evergreen culture, there are many valleys full of blossoms and free-flowing rivers. You can also buy freshly plugged fruits to savour. Some of the Nagaland famous places to visit in Wokh are Totsu Cliff, Tehurang Valley, Doyang river and Mt Tiyi.


  6. Khonoma Green Village




    7. One of the most untouched beauty of Nagaland, Khonoma Green village is a result of continuous responsible living. Serene views, fresh air, the calm and peaceful environment makes this village a wonderful holiday destination and one of the popular Nagaland tourist places. Everyone house that you will see here is built from scratch with only recycled and natural raw materials. Home to Angami tribe, who earns their daily bread from agriculture, weaving and handlooms. This village has become a great example of community development and sustainable living. Some of the best tourist attraction here are Naga Heritage Village, Dovipie Inn and Damant Tomb.


  7. Benreu



    8. v

    If you want to go totally off-road and visit someplace which offers complete solitude then Benreu is one of the best tourist places in Nagaland for you to visit. It is one of the most unexplored villages with 180 families residing according to the 2020 report. Home to another unique tribe called Zeliang, Benreu is a place with rich flora and fauna. It is located at an elevation of 1950m and nestled in Mt Pauna. Because of its serene and cool atmosphere, it is one of the finest tourist places in Nagaland.


  8. Meluri




    9. Situated in the Kiphire district, lies a small village called Meluri. This place well known for the hunting and hunting skills of the inhabitants here. It is home to the Pochury tribe of Nagaland. Explore the dignified rock formations and visit Dzudu Lake, Shilloi Lake and Zanibu peak from where you can a magnificent view of the mighty Mt. Everest along with the far-stretching valleys of Nagaland. Another speciality is the furniture made up of cane and brine salt.


  9. Tuensang




    10. The village of Tuensang is known for the producing most gorgeous handloom, Naga jewellery, various handicrafts and fantastic art pieces. You can also experience the culture and tradition of the co-living tribes of Tuensang. And if you’re interested to know the history of the Naga tribes and everything related to them, visit the Living Stone, located at a distance of 60km from the Tuensang town.


  10. Longleng




    11. Home to Phom Nagas earns their daily bread from pottery, spinning and making different handicrafts with bamboo. They also take a lot of pride in their culture and celebrate vibrantly. The village gives a feel of an ancient and mystical vibe full of natural beauty and wonder. Famous among the thrill-seekers and motorist, the journey to Longleng is full of surprises. There are many small and quaint markets around the village from where you can buy wonderful artefacts made by the Phom Nagas.


  11. Kiphire




    12. Kiphire is one of the best places to visit in Nagaland if you’re seeking some adventure. It is the highest peak in Nagaland – Saramati with an elevation of 895m. Put on your hiking shoes and start exploring awe-inspiring virgin nature across the Rhododendron forest. Additionally, there are other adventurous tourist places of Nagaland like Caver’s delight, Lover’s paradise and Wawade.


  12. Zunheboto




    13. One of the most colourful tribes of Nagaland is the Sumi Nagas. This is the tribe that is significantly responsible for introducing martial arts to the Naga community. If you are interested in the historical monuments of Nagaland, you must visit the Baptist Church which is the largest in all of Asia, a hamlet and a sanctuary that is home to many endangered species of birds and animals. Keltomi Village, Bird Sanctuary and Satoi range are some of the best places to visit in Zunheboto.


  13. Phek




    14. Phek is an astounding place for bird watcher and nature lovers. This is one of the prettiest districts of Nagaland with several lakes, rivers and lush greenery all around. One can enjoy the beauty according to their liking i.e., trek, hike or birdwatching. And if you want to get up close and personal with the inhabitants of Phek, visit Khezakeno heritage village.


  14. Dzukou Village




    15. It is by far the most well-known and top tourist attraction in Nagaland. With an elevation of 2462m, Dzukou valley is the most picturesque place in Nagaland. The valley is full of lilies which perfectly complements the green valley and borders Manipur. This valley is an ideal place for camping and other adventurous activities.


  15. Touphema Village




    16. For people who seek to know about different culture and traditions and be a part of them, Touphema Village is one of the best famous tourist places in Nagaland. Try authentic Naga culture and cuisines and drinks like rice beer. With an age-old charm of the village, indulge yourself with the inhabitants and listen to some interesting stories associated with the village. You can also opt to stay at the huts to get the best experience of the local life.


  16. Naga Heritage Village




    17. The history related to the Nagas and about Nagaland can be discovered here at Naga Heritage Village, also called Kisama Heritage Village. The place offers to protect and preserve the ethnicities of the Naga tribal communities, making it a paradise for art lovers and history buffs. If you’re visiting during the month of December, you can witness the beautiful and charismatic Hornbill Festival.


  17. Pfutsero




    18. One of the coldest places in Nagaland is Pfutsero with an elevation of 2133m above sea level. You can see the glorious “Glory peak” and get awestruck. Although, it gets really cold during the winters the spirit of the local remains high for celebrating Christmas. One can get the see the pretty cherry blossoms in March-April. Tsukhenye is one of the greatest festivals of the Chakesang tribe of Pfutsero.


  18. Japfu Peak




    19. With an elevation of 3084m, it makes the second-highest peak of Nagaland. Afar from the bustle of the city and into the womb of nature, one can spend the best time of their life. A heavenly place for thrill-seekers and in general nature lovers. This place is also known as the “Valley of Flowers” of Northeast India. Spend some time in peace and click some great snaps for Instagram.


  19. Kachari Ruins




    20. Also called Dimasa Kachari Ruins, looks something like a Hobbit land. The houses here look like a mushroom. It is believed to have emerged around the 13th century during the Dimasa Kachari reign. Again, one of the best places to visit in Nagaland if you want to go offbeat. Kachari ruins are the perfect place for those who love to know about the history of a place. Furthermore, the tall grass and unkempt mosses around the ruins is a cherry on top. You can also visit the Nagaland Science Centre, Dieziphe Village and Hong Kong market for shopping.


  20. Chumukedmia Village




    21. A small hill station with a bird’s eye view of the Dimapur district. An ideal place to visit with your partner to enjoy some quality time where no one is going to disturb you. Explore the lustrous waterfall and raw wilderness of the place. Some of the best places to visit in Chumuledima village is Triple Falls and Nagaland Zoological Park.
